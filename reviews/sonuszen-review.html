The Tinnitus Problem No One Talks About

Over 50 million Americans experience tinnitus — the constant ringing, buzzing, or hissing that no one else can hear. Most doctors offer the same frustrating answer: "Learn to live with it." Hearing aids mask symptoms. White noise machines provide temporary distraction. But almost nothing addresses the root cause.

Emerging neuroscience research points to a breakdown in the neural signaling between the cochlea (inner ear) and the auditory cortex in the brain. When this connection misfires, the brain generates phantom sounds to compensate. This is why tinnitus is now classified as a neurological problem, not just an ear problem.
