The Grid Threat That Most Preparedness Guides Ignore

Traditional emergency preparedness guides focus on natural disasters — hurricanes, earthquakes, blizzards. These are real threats worth preparing for. But there is an increasingly documented, less-discussed category of grid risk that has emerged over the past decade: the digital and AI-related attack surface of modern power infrastructure.

Today's power grid is not the isolated mechanical system of the 1970s. It is partially managed by automated supervisory control and data acquisition (SCADA) systems, AI-optimized load balancing software, and network-connected infrastructure that spans thousands of nodes. Security researchers, NERC (the North American Electric Reliability Corporation), and the US Department of Homeland Security have all publicly documented growing concerns about the cyber and AI-related vulnerabilities this creates.
